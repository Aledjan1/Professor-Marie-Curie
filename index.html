Professor Marie Curie

Contributed to the development of nuclear medicine and radiology.

Brief biography of Marie Curie

  • 1867 - Born November 7 in Warsaw, Poland.
  • 1891 - Moved to Paris to study physics and mathematics at the Sorbonne.
  • 1895 - Married Pierre Curie.
  • 1898 - Discovered the elements polonium and radium.
  • 1903 - Received the Nobel Prize in Physics jointly with Pierre Curie and Henri Becquerel.
  • 1906 - Pierre Curie died; Maria became the first woman professor at the Sorbonne.
  • 1911 - Awarded the Nobel Prize in Chemistry for her study of radium and polonium.
  • 1914 - Founded the Curie Institute in Paris.
  • 1934 - Died on July 4 from aplastic anemia caused by exposure to radiation.
